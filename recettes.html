Mille-feuille chocolat framboise

Level Preparation Cooking Price Quantity
Medium 1h30 None Cheap 6 persons
Ingredients Equipment used
351g of dark chocolate clingfilm (width: 30cm)
2 baskets of fresh raspberries non-stick plate 40x30cm
1 branch of fresh mint spatula type maryse (25cm)
3dl of crème fraiche with a fat level of 30% curved spatula 15cm
70g of icing sugar piping bag (40cm)

  1. To realise this recipe begin by preparing all the ingredients.
  2. Wrap of clingfilm the non-stick plate 40x30cm.
  3. In a bowl, put the chocolate. Melt it a few minutes in the micro-wave at a moderate power.
  4. Mix withe the spatula type maryse in order to get melted chocolate without lump.
  5. Pour the melted chocolate on the non-stick plate and spread it with the curved spatula.
  6. Put the non-stick plate in the fridge.

  • At the same time, whisk the crème fraîche as whipped cream.
  • At the end of the whisking, add 50g of icing glace.
  • Chisel subtly the branches' leaves of mint.
  • Add this mint to the chantilly.
  • Mix it.
  • Fill the piping bag and put it in the fridge.
  • Get the chocolate patch out of the fridge when it's hard.
  • Trim the broders of the patch in order to get distincts borders.
  • Cut the chocolate in square. Predict 3 squares by mille-feuille.
  • Place the first square in the middle of a plate.
  • With the piping bag make some roses of chantilly with the mint.
  • Pick some fresh raspberries on it.
  • Cover with an other square of chocolate.
  • Make some roses of chantilly again.
  • Then, pick some fresh raspberries.
  • Dust the last square of chocolate.
  • Put it on the rasberries.
  • Decorate the top with a rose of chantilly and a leaf of fresh mint.





    • Gratin Dauphinois

    Level Preparation Cooking Price Quantity
    Easy 1h 15min Cheap 4 persons
    Ingredients Equipment used
    800g of potatoes Japanese mandolin 6,5cm
    30cl of whole milk Saucepan 28cm
    50cl of crème fraîche Garlic cut
    a big knob of butter Spatula
    2 garlic cloves circle hollow platter in black cast hiron
    Nutmeg
    Salt
    Pepper

    1. To realise this recipe begin by preparing all the ingredients and preheat the oven.
    2. Wash, peel and slice thinly the potatoes with the Japanese mandolin.
    3. Place it in the saucepan with milk, crème fraîche, garlic, salt, pepper, butter and nutmeg.
    4. Bring it to the boil.
    5. Decrease the burner and pursue the firing during 8 minutes. Stir in it with a spatula.
    6. Put the potatoes in the platter. Flatten the top and let it cool down.
    7. At the same time, add 20cl of crème fraîche.
    8. Put it in the oven at 180°C and let it bake during around 15 minutes.
    9. Then serve it in the platter.




    Carpaccio de St-Jacques

    Level Preparation Cooking Price Quantity
    Easy 20min None Quite expensive 4 persons
    Ingredients Equipment used
    12 knobs of Saint Jacques A blender
    orange juice A big bowl
    lemon juice A strainer
    2 spoons of nut oil Spatula
    4 spoons of Colza oil circle hollow platter in black cast hiron
    mix of bays
    1/2 bunch of chives
    salt, pepper

    1. To realise this recipe begin by preparing a marinade.
    2. In a blender, grind a spoon of bays.
    3. In the big bowl, mix the juices, ground bays and oils.
    4. Finish the marinade by slating and peppering. Emulsify the mixing (put 30min in the fridge).
    5. Clean the scallops. Cut them in slices and place them in individual plates.
    6. Slice thinly the scallops, pour the marinade and scatter with some chives. It's ready !
